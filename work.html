Arduino School

The Arduino workshop offers participants a hands-on experience with one of the most versatile and popular open-source electronics platforms available today. In this workshop, participants will delve into the world of Arduino, which is widely used for prototyping and creating interactive electronic projects.

Throughout the workshop, participants will learn the basics of Arduino programming and electronics, starting from understanding the components and their functions to writing and uploading code to control various sensors, actuators, and other electronic devices. The workshop typically covers topics such as:

1 . Introduction to Arduino: Participants will learn about the Arduino board, its components, and the Arduino Integrated Development Environment (IDE).
2 . Basic Electronics: Understanding fundamental electronic components such as resistors, LEDs, switches, and sensors, and how to connect them to the Arduino board.
3 . Arduino Programming: Learning the basics of programming with Arduino using the C/C++ programming language, including variables, functions, loops, and conditional statements.
4 . Sensor Interfacing: Exploring how to interface various sensors such as temperature sensors, ultrasonic sensors, and motion sensors with the Arduino board.
5 . Actuator Control: Controlling output devices like LEDs, motors, and relays using the Arduino board.
6 . Project Building: Participants will work on hands-on projects throughout the workshop, applying the knowledge gained to create interactive electronic systems and prototypes.
7 . Troubleshooting and Debugging: Learning how to identify and resolve common issues that may arise during Arduino projects.

By the end of the workshop, participants will have gained practical experience in building and programming Arduino-based projects, empowering them to continue exploring the exciting world of electronics and prototyping on their own. Whether beginners or experienced hobbyists, participants will leave the workshop equipped with the skills and confidence to embark on their own Arduino projects and experiments.
